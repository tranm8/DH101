Introduction

Welcome to my DH101 portfolio for Critical Making in the Age of AI. This website collects my creative projects, reflections, and final thoughts from the semester. Through critical making, I learned that artificial intelligence is not just a neutral tool that helps people work faster. AI is shaped by human choices, training data, social values, hidden labor, environmental costs, and systems of power.

My central argument is that being human in the age of AI means learning how to create with technology while also questioning it. AI can generate images, text, code, and ideas, but it cannot fully replace human judgment, responsibility, memory, emotion, or ethical decision-making. In this course, making became a way for me to understand AI more critically. Each project asked me to think about who gets represented, who gets erased, who does the invisible work, and what consequences are hidden behind digital tools.

My makes explore topics such as identity, storytelling, remix culture, mapping, networks, labor, ecology, and future human-AI relationships. Together, they show that digital creation is never only technical. It is also cultural, political, environmental, and deeply human. This portfolio argues that responsible AI use requires transparency, care, revision, and critical reflection.

Central Argument: Being human in the age of AI means using technology creatively, but also critically, ethically, and responsibly.
