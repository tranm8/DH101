Introduction

Welcome to my DH101 portfolio for Critical Making in the Age of AI. Across this semester, I explored how artificial intelligence changes the meaning of making, creativity, identity, and responsibility. Through projects such as an AI selfie, comic, map, network visualization, bot, and interactive game, I learned that AI is not simply a neutral tool. It is shaped by human choices, training data, design systems, labor, and environmental costs.

My central argument is that being human in the age of AI means learning how to work with technology critically rather than passively. AI can help us generate ideas, images, text, and code, but it also reflects bias, hides labor, and can make digital creation seem effortless when it is actually built on many human and material systems. Critical making helped me understand that the process matters as much as the final product. Each make became a way to ask questions: Who created this? Who is missing? What work is invisible? What values are built into the tool?

This website collects my major makes, weekly reflections, AI use statement, accessibility and sustainability statement, and final reflective essay. Together, these pages show my growth as a student learning to use AI with creativity, transparency, and ethical awareness.
