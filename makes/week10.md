# Week 10 – Games & Play

## The Artifact

[AI Boss Interview](../AI boss interview.html)

## Design Statement

My game, AI boss interview, is an interactive Twine narrative about leadership in an AI-driven corporate world. The player takes the role of a CEO candidate being evaluated by an AI boss. At first, the interview appears to be about business skill and fast decision-making, but the deeper question is whether leadership should be reduced to optimization, speed, and data. The game asks: when AI systems become more powerful and persuasive, what kinds of judgment should remain human?

Rather than simply presenting the player with two options, one good and one bad, the game presents different choices that challenge the player's ability to balance four key components: profitability, public trust, job creation, and accountability. The game presents many choices to the player including either removing a forest for a short term gain, destroying magnets state funds, or retaining an effective but racially biased employment algorithm. The final choice, whether or not to turn over all major decisions made by a corporation to AI, is the most important choice the player makes in the game because it clearly states the primary argument made by the game. To be successful in the game, the player must reject both inactivity and mindless compliance with automation 100% of the time.

The mechanics support this theme through procedural rhetoric. The rules of the game reward players who treat AI as a tool for analysis rather than a substitute for moral and political judgment. Players lose if they avoid all risk, but they also lose if they choose efficiency without ethics. In this way, the branching structure argues that leadership is not about always maximizing profit or always avoiding harm; it is about accepting responsibility for complex decisions.

This design was influenced by ideas from Critical Making in the Age of AI, especially the idea that Twine can be used to construct choice and argument through simulation and representation, not just tell a story. I wanted the game’s structure itself to make the argument that human judgment still matters in the age of AI.

## Artist Statement

This project helped me understand that games are different from static stories because they make ideas felt through rules, choices, and consequences. If I wrote AI Interview as a regular essay or short story, I could still talk about ethics, labor, and AI governance, but the reader would not experience those tensions in the same way. In the game, the player has to move through pressure, uncertainty, and consequence. That interactive structure makes the theme more immediate.

One of the biggest things I learned is that games can make arguments procedurally. In my game, the player cannot win by being purely profit-driven, but they also cannot win by refusing every difficult decision. The system pushes the player toward a more complex position: AI can assist leadership, but it cannot replace human accountability. That argument is built into the structure of the game, not just the dialogue.

Working in Twine also showed me how strong even simple branching can be. I originally thought the project only needed a few short decisions, but I realized that meaningful branching matters more than just having many links. Adding press reactions and board reactions helped each choice feel more real and showed how public image, corporate power, and ethics are always connected.

This project also made me think more critically about AI collaboration. AI was useful for support, especially for brainstorming options, revising wording, and identifying weak or fake choices. But the most important work was deciding what argument I wanted the game to make and which revisions fit that argument. If I continued developing this project, I would expand the consequences further and make the endings feel even more distinct. 

## Attribution & AI Use
How I use AI: 

What AI helped me do

Identify weak or “fake” choices in my early Twine draft
Suggest ways to deepen consequences after major decisions
Explain how to structure Twine passages and endings more effectively
Adjust grammar and wording. 

What I kept

I kept the core concept of an AI interviewing a human for a CEO role. I also kept my central theme: the tension between profit, ethics, and human judgment. The final game still reflects my own interest in how AI changes leadership, responsibility, and decision-making.

What I changed

I changed several of the original branches after getting feedback. In particular, I revised choices that felt too simple, added more consequences after major decisions, and made the endings more distinct. I also added press reactions and board reactions to make each choice feel more meaningful.

My policy on AI use

I believe AI is most useful for brainstorming, editing, and critique. It can help me notice gaps, but it should not replace my own argument, voice, or design choices. For this assignment, I used AI to support revision and reflection, while keeping the project’s final direction and meaning under my control.
