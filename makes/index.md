# Museum of Makes

## A Critical Making Gallery

This gallery collects my major “Makes” from DH101: *Critical Making in the Age of AI*. Each project explores how AI shapes identity, storytelling, space, knowledge, labor, power, ethics, and ecology.

Instead of presenting these works as a simple list of assignments, I imagine this page as a small digital exhibition. Each Make is a different entry point into my larger question: **What does it mean to be human in the age of AI?**

---

## Gallery

### [The Archive as an Argument](week01.html)

A reverse-engineering project about Photogrammar and how historical photographs become searchable data, visual knowledge, and public memory.

---

### [When AI Makes Me Too Perfect](week03.html)

A selfie experiment about AI, beauty, identity, and the loss of human imperfections.

---

### [The Everyday Joke Machine](week04.html)

A comic based on everyday Vietnamese conversation, friendship, humor, and digital storytelling.

---

### [AI’s Death Geography](week07.html)

An interactive map tracing how AI depends on minerals, water, data centers, labor, and hidden infrastructure.

---

### [Cartography of the Invisible](week08.html)

A network visualization showing how AI is connected to corporate power, resource extraction, invisible labor, and environmental cost.

---

### [The AI Boss Interview](week10.html)

A Twine game about leadership, automation, profit, ethics, and human accountability in an AI-driven corporate world.

---

### [The Cloud Has a Body](week12.html)

A visual research artifact about AI, ecology, extraction, water, energy, and the hidden material costs behind the “cloud.”
