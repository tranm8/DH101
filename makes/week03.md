# When AI Makes Me Too Perfect

*A critical selfie experiment about AI, beauty, identity, and the loss of human imperfections.*

---

## The Make

This artifact shows two AI-generated selfies created from my original image. The goal was to test how AI interprets my face, identity, and beauty.

## My Take

In the edited image, I find that my nose and hair are the parts the AI rendered most accurately. Since my nose takes up a large portion of my face, it initially created a sense of familiarity for me when I saw the generated image. The AI captured the relative proportions between my eyes and nose, making my distinctive features clearer within the facial structure.

However, I find a significant weakness in the AI’s ability to create a person's “soul” or aura. I completely fail to see myself in the soul-less perfection that the AI imposed on the photo. The algorithm standardized my beauty, automatically erasing wrinkles, making the skin extremely smooth, and correcting the natural asymmetries of my face.

In this, I see a clear limitation of AI: it does not understand the value of flaws — the specific characteristics that have become my identity, such as the scar near my eye from being scratched by a classmate when I was a child. I no longer have the imperfections that make me who I am. This image does not reflect me; instead, it reflects a biased machine assumption of what a face “should” look like to be considered attractive.

## Attribution & AI Use

### Tools Used

DALL-E

### Prompts Summary

I used an actual photo of myself as the basis for the model and gave it a simple prompt: “Based on the face of the girl in this photo, create a selfie of me.” After the first result, I gave it more detailed descriptions of my features to make the image feel closer to my actual appearance.

### What the AI Generated

The AI created a digital selfie where my hair and general style were recognized quickly. However, my facial features looked extremely “fake” and lacked the authenticity of a natural human face.

### What I Changed or Decided

I chose to interact more directly with the AI by describing specific features that I wanted it to preserve. I told the AI that my nose is larger at the tip, that I have hidden double eyelids, and that my upper lip is thicker than my lower lip. I did this to push the AI beyond its default “pretty girl” template and closer to my own unique physical structure.
