## Process Documentation

To explore the intersection of algorithmic generation and human curation, I built a generative
music system using Suno AI. My initial goal was to recreate the specific emotional resonance
of a nostalgic anime opening. I established my base "rules" with a prompt asking for a
"sentimental J-Pop anime opening style" featuring an "upbeat but bittersweet melody," a
"funky bassline inspired by 'Lemon'," and "high energy, crisp production."

Once the base system was established, I introduced systematic variations to test the AI’s
boundaries and control specific parameters. First, I altered the genre parameter, changing
"J-Pop" to "Acoustic City Pop" to see how the algorithmic arrangement would shift the
instrumentation. Next, I manipulated the thematic parameters by explicitly requesting lyrics
about a boy and a girl who share the same niche jokes and sense of humor, aiming for deeper
narrative content.

In total, I generated 9 tracks. Even with a relatively small volume, this process quickly
revealed the "exhaustion of novelty" inherent in generative systems. Without strict human
curation, the AI's outputs often defaulted to a safe but boring "averageness." I explicitly
rejected seven of the generated tracks because they were entirely too formulaic; their
melodies just flowed "stably" without any dynamic focus, emotional peaks, or structural
surprises.

I ultimately curated the outputs down to two final tracks. I selected "Love or lemon" because
the system surprised me with a perfectly placed rhythmic shift in the chorus and
professional-sounding strings. For "Same Sky, Same Joke" the AI delivered incredibly
authentic vocals with strong emotional impact. Interestingly, manipulating the prompt for
deeper lyrics led to a fascinating algorithmic glitch: the AI accidentally incorporated the
wrong language. This unexpected error highlighted the tension between my controlled
parameters and the unpredictable nature of machine generation.

## Artist Statement

When AI can generate endlessly, creativity changes. It is no longer just about making things;
it is about curating them. "Pure" AI creativity is just math and probability. If left alone, AI
usually creates things that are safe and boring (this is the averageness).

My human agency in this project happened in two steps: writing the prompts and choosing
the best songs. I gave the AI my specific idea: a bittersweet story about two people sharing
the same jokes. Then, I faced the exhaustion of novelty. Most of the AI's songs were too
normal and lacked real emotion.

I decided a song was "good" when it surprised me. I chose "Love or lemon" because of its
unexpected rhythm change. More importantly, I chose "Same Sky, Same Joke" because the
AI made a mistake and used the wrong language. This mistake became a happy accident. It
proved that while machines can make endless songs, only a human can listen to a mistake,
find meaning in it, and call it art. My creativity is how I choose and put limits on the AI.
