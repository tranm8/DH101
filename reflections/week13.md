# Week 13 Reflection

Reflect 13: Do we imagine AI futures as utopian, dystopian, or something in between? What do those visions reveal about us?

I see the future as a messy "in-between". Our tendency to imagine AI as a weightless, clean savior reveals a collective denial about the material weight of technology and the scars on the earth it leaves behind. While we talk about the abstract Cloud my research into Congolese cobalt and invisible labor shows that this future is actually built on the ancient bedrock of resource extraction and systemic inequality. This vision exposes that we are currently more comfortable searching for technological fixes than we are confronting the uncomfortable truth that our digital comfort is often built on the physical suffering of others. Ultimately, the future of AI reminds us that we must choose to see the human and environmental costs behind the screen if we want to build a world that is truly fair and sustainable for everyone.
