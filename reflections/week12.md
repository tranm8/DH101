# Week 12 Reflection

Reflect 12: Is AI sustainable? What ecological trade-offs are we willing to accept?

The relationship between AI and ecology is a high-stakes paradox where technology acts as both a potential lifesaver and a significant environmental drain. On one hand, AI serves as a powerful ally in the fight against climate change by optimizing renewable energy grids, tracking deforestation with precision, and discovering new materials for carbon capture. On the other hand, the physical reality of AI is incredibly resource-intensive, requiring massive amounts of electricity and millions of gallons of freshwater to cool the servers that power every query. To resolve this tension, the industry must transition toward "Green AI" by prioritizing energy-efficient hardware and smaller, specialized models that run on carbon-free power. Ultimately, if we do not manage the material cost of our digital growth, we risk damaging the very planet we are using artificial intelligence to save.
