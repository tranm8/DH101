# Week 1 Reflection

Reflect 1: What counts as “making” in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

Previously, “making” was often associated with humans creating and working by hand or mind. But in the age of AI, AI can create products without humans directly doing the work. So the question raised here is, who is the one “making”? If you give commands to AI, select, and edit the results, then you are considered the one “making” in a directorial way. In general, “making” now is not just about creating by hand, but also about thinking, guiding, and deciding. When AI participates in production, “making” becomes faster because AI helps us experiment with ideas very quickly. AI helps people who do not yet have skills to also be able to create products, making products more diverse because there is a combination between humans and AI. But besides that, there are also things lost when there is AI participation in product production. Products made by AI will lack the story and human effort. More seriously besides that, because it is made by AI, who will be the person responsible if AI creates errors or plagiarism? In the AI era, it cannot be denied that AI helps us expand our capabilities, but we must rethink the issues of creativity, ownership, and the meaning of the product.
