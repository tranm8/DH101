# Week 7 Reflection

Reflect 7: How does AI reshape global geographies of power, labor, and environment?

AI reshapes global geographies of power, labor, and environment by concentrating technological power in a few countries and major tech companies that control data, infrastructure, and innovation. This means that the benefits of AI, such as profit, speed, and technological influence, are often centered in wealthy regions, while the costs are spread across other places. This shifts global labor patterns as some jobs become automated while new technology-focused jobs grow in specific regions. However, many hidden forms of labor, such as data labeling, content moderation, mineral extraction, and maintenance work, are often performed by workers who remain invisible in the final AI product. At the same time, AI systems rely on large data centers that consume significant energy and water, creating environmental pressures in the places where these technologies are developed and operated. Overall, AI changes global geography by creating an uneven system: power and profit are concentrated in tech centers, while labor demands and environmental burdens are distributed across different communities and ecosystems.
