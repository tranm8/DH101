# Week 11 Reflection

Reflect 11: Who does the invisible work of AI, and who profits from it?

The invisible work of AI is primarily performed by millions of ghost workers in the Global South who handle the essential, often traumatic, tasks of data labeling, content moderation, and reinforcement learning. These individuals operate as precarious gig laborers with minimal job security or benefits, while the vast majority of the economic value is extracted by Big Tech giants, AI labs, and hardware manufacturers who own the underlying infrastructure and proprietary models. Ultimately, this system relies on a hidden human supply chain that concentrates wealth among a few dominant firms and investors while the workers at the base remain largely uncompensated for the long-term value they create.
