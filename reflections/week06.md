# Week 6 Reflection

Reflect 6: What do we gain/lose when machines “read” literature for us?

When we let machines read for us, we gain distant reading power, allowing us to use statistical density to find objective patterns, like word frequencies, that a human eye might miss. Although,  AI can perform a qualitative synthesis to explain themes, it lacks genuine intuition, shifting the meaning from a soulful conversation to a series of semantic connections and data points. Therefor, we lose the human pulse of the story, as machines remain context-blind to the emotional weight, irony, and sensory experiences that define literature.
