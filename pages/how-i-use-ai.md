## How I Use AI

In DH101, I used AI as an assistant, not as a replacement for my own thinking. I used tools such as ChatGPT, DALL-E, GitHub Copilot, and other digital platforms to brainstorm, draft, code, and revise. However, I made the final decisions about meaning, design, argument, and ethics.

## Example 1: AI Selfie

For my AI selfie project, I used an image generator to create a version of myself based on a photo and written description. The result looked polished, but it also erased many details that make my face feel real to me. I revised the prompt and reflected on how AI often follows default beauty standards. This helped me understand that AI can reproduce bias even when it seems creative.

## Example 2: Website Coding

I used AI tools to help me understand HTML, Markdown, GitHub Pages links, and folder structure. AI helped me troubleshoot problems such as 404 errors and broken navigation. However, I still had to test each page, check the links, revise the layout, and decide what content belonged in the final portfolio.

## Limits and Bias

AI sometimes gives answers that sound confident but are incomplete or too general. It can also ignore context, reproduce bias, or make design choices that do not fit the assignment. Because of this, I treated AI output as a draft, not as finished work.

## Ethical Use

I documented when I used AI, credited tools in my attribution boxes, and separated what AI generated from what I created or edited. For me, responsible AI use means being transparent, checking outputs carefully, and keeping human judgment at the center of the work.
